About Us

You’re probably wondering right now: Is Lola Anne real? The answer is… well, sort of. “Lola Anne” refers to Anne Del Rosario Tikbalang—known for her legendary ability to cook anything with just three ingredients and a lot of unsolicited life advice. Co-owner Tiffy San Antonio’s great-great-aunt on her mother’s cousin’s side, Lola Anne is the mythical force behind the brand.

Lola Anne's

Siopao Tostado: Lola Anne's Bold Twist

In 2016, Lola Anne’s spiced things up with their take on the iconic Siopao Tostado. Inspired by the Bicolano favorite, they gave it a twist: softer bread, a hint of spice, and a filling that’s basically sisig in bread form. Tiffy proudly calls it “the bad boy of siopao” because it’s bold, spicy, and doesn’t care about your feelings. Students in España became instant fans, with some claiming it was “life-changing, parang nakamove-on na rin ako.”

Cheese Overload The Rise of Lola Anne's Donuts

Tiffy, never one to back down from a challenge, went full Triple Cheese mode. “Three times the cheese, three times the happiness, pero twice the food coma!” she jokes. The post-pandemic craze for these donuts was “insane,” according to Tiffy. “Parang bawat tao gusto bumili kahit wala nang stock! Naghahanap na sila sa black market!”

Today, Lola Anne’s produces up to 50,000 donuts a day—enough to cover both breakfast cravings and emotional breakdowns.
